Difference between Paraphrasing and
Summarizing
_________________________________________


Summarizing and paraphrasing are two techniques that can be used for different purposes.For example,
you can summarize a piece of text if you want to make it a little shorter for your audience. On the other
hand, you can paraphrase some content if you want to improve the flow and clarity.

Now, since these two are both the names of techniques (and they are both used in writing), it can be
a little confusing to know how exactly they’re different.

In this blog post, we will be looking at some of the main differences between these two. To make the
whole concept clearer, we will also show some examples using sample text.

What is Summarizing?

In the words of Merriam-Webster, the definition of summarizing is as follows:
“…to tell in or reduce to a summary…”
Of course, this itself is not very descriptive. To understand the definition a bit better, we have to
understand what a summary is itself. And for that, the following answer is provided by Merriam-Webster:
“…an abstract, abridgment, or compendium especially of a preceding discourse…”
De-cluttering the complexity a little, we can understand a summary to be a shortened form of a piece of
content. The process of summarizing means shortening something in such a way that the main points are
encapsulated.

What are Some Uses of Summarizing?

Summarizing is a useful skill that is required in different situations and instances. To name a few, here are
some uses of summarizing.
1. To create an excerpt or short summary for a blog post
2. To write an abstract of a research paper
3.To write a synopsis of an essay or an assignment
4.To create meta descriptions and other types of small-form content





What is Paraphrasing?

Paraphrasing is the act of rephrasing or restating a sentence, passage, or entire text while retaining the
original meaning. It involves expressing the content in different words or sentence structures to convey
the same ideas or information in a clearer or more concise manner. Paraphrasing is commonly used to
avoid plagiarism, to simplify complex language, or to provide an alternative way of expressing a concept.

Effective paraphrasing requires a deep understanding of the original text's context and meaning. It's
important to accurately capture the essence of the content while using different vocabulary and
sentence structures. Paraphrased content should be coherent, grammatically correct, and faithful to
the source's intent.

Paraphrasing can be a valuable skill in various contexts, including academic writing, research, content
creation, and communication. It allows writers to present information in their own voice while maintaining
the integrity of the original ideas.

What are Some Uses of Paraphrasing?

Paraphrasing is a versatile and valuable skill that is used in a variety of contexts for different purposes. Here
are some common uses of paraphrasing:
1. Technical writers paraphrase complex technical information to make it more accessible to a broader
audience without sacrificing accuracy.
2. Paraphrasing can be employed in legal documents to simplify complex legal jargon while maintaining the
legal intent and meaning.
3. Translators use paraphrasing to accurately convey the meaning of phrases or sentences from one language
to another.
4.Paraphrasing is a tool for editors and proofreaders to improve sentence structure, coherence, and clarity in
written content.
